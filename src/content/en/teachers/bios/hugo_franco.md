Hugo Franco is one of the leading guitarists on the contemporary Spanish folk scene, as well as a multi-instrumentalist, producer, and teacher, with a career shaped by the meeting of traditional music, contemporary folk, and world musics. He began training very young at Fiddle Camps in the United States, immersing himself in fiddle tradition and American roots music, and in classes with **Xosé Liz** at E-Trad in Vigo, which opened a decisive path toward a deep understanding of traditional Galician music.

That dual training—Galicia and the wider world—produced a distinctive sound world, nourished by Irish, Scottish, American, bluegrass, Breton, and other international folk styles. Traditional Galician music has stayed at the core of his artistic work, the ground where he has grown most and where he has invested most of his musical life. At the same time he has explored musics from China, Mexico, Costa Rica, Morocco, and Brazil, with a particular focus on Brazilian forró and the huapango of the Mexican Huasteca, both of which have strongly shaped his rhythmic and creative approach.

As a performer he has been involved in projects such as **Entre Trastes**, **Gaizca**, **Dadga**, and **Galicia Fiddle**, collaborating with international artists including **Casey Driessen**, **Marta Roma**, **Marc Xarau**, and **Roo Geddes**. He has also developed solo projects, starting with the EP *If It Feels Good Then It Must Be*, which highlights his more intimate side as composer and producer. Alongside that he has formed lasting duos: with **Marc Xarau** he toured the Balkans and released the EP *АЛКОХОЛ ЦИГАРИ*, rooted in first-hand research into Balkan music; and with Scottish fiddler **Roo Geddes** he created the duo **Darach**, appearing at festivals such as Celtic Connections (Scotland), the Lorient Interceltic Festival (France), and the Ortigueira Festival (Spain), as well as teaching masterclasses at NAFCO and **Crisol de Cuerda**.

He teaches workshops in Europe and Latin America (technique, traditional repertoire, improvisation, and accompaniment). In production he bridges roots acoustic music and trap in the Galician urban scene. He remains active with **Darach** and with **Marc Xarau**, with new releases and tours in the pipeline.
