Élisabeth Giroux is a cellist from Quebec whose work crosses classical, jazz, folk and traditional music. She began cello at 13, completed her studies at the Conservatoire de musique de Montréal with a Prix avec grande distinction, and later studied jazz and folk/traditional approaches with **Eugene Friesen** and **Natalie Haas** at Berklee College of Music. She has performed with **Angèle Dubeau et La Pietà**, the dance company **La La La Human Steps**, **Sacré Tympan**, the **Orchestre national de jazz de Montréal** and artists such as **Chromeo** and **Luce Dufault**.

In 2015 she co-founded **É.T.É** with fiddler **Élisabeth Moquin** and bouzouki player **Thierry Clouette**, a neo-traditional trio that arranges Québécois and Acadian repertoire and original pieces with classical, jazz and progressive rock colours. With **É.T.É** she has released *Le boire des minuits* (2017), *Les quatre roses* (2019) and *Sur ces eaux* (2022); the group has toured in Canada, the United States, Europe and Australia, and received honours including a Création Aldor award, an OPUS for Discovery of the Year (2017–2018), the People's Choice prize at Cabaret festif (2018), a Linda Lemay award at the Festival international de la chanson de Granby (2019) and a Lucien Award from GAMIQ (2020).

She co-founded the **Coopérative des professeurs de musique** in Montreal and has taught cello there since 2009.
