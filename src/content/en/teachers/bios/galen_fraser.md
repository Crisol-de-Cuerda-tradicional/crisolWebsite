Galen Fraser is an American-Scottish fiddler, composer, producer, and traditional music pedagogue from the San Juan Ridge in Northern California. A graduate of Berklee College of Music with a degree in songwriting, Fraser has developed a distinctive voice on the fiddle that blends Scottish, American, and European traditional music with contemporary composition.

His debut solo album, *Mischief Managed* (2016), features original music rooted in traditional styles while expanding the expressive possibilities of the instrument. Strings Magazine wrote: “Fraser's fiddling entwines the robust crunch and skirl of the old world with the airy ostinato and shimmering tremolo of the new.”

Fraser has also worked as a composer for Facebook, creating Spanish pop music for the company’s sound library. He performs internationally with the duo **San Miguel Fraser**, a collaboration with Spanish violinist **María San Miguel** that explores the meeting point between Celtic and Castilian traditions.

In addition to his performing career, Fraser is an active traditional music pedagogue and community builder. He is the artistic director of **Crisol de Cuerda** and serves as vice president of the Castilla Fiddle Association, working to foster traditional music education and collaboration across Spain and beyond.

The son of renowned Scottish fiddler **Alasdair Fraser**, Galen Fraser is currently based in Spain, where he continues to build bridges between musical traditions through performance, teaching, and cultural exchange.
