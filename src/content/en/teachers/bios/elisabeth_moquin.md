Élisabeth Moquin is a fiddle player from Saint-Majorique-de-Grantham, Quebec, where she first encountered traditional arts. She holds a diploma in traditional fiddle from the music program at Cégep de Joliette. She co-founded **É.T.É**—a trio with bouzouki player **Thierry Clouette** and cellist **Élisabeth Giroux** that weaves Québécois and Acadian traditions together with jazz, progressive rock and classical colours—and the group **SAMEDI**. With **É.T.É** she has released _Le boire des minuits_ (2017), _Les quatre roses_ (2019) and _Sur ces eaux_ (2022). The trio has earned recognition including a Création Aldor award, an OPUS for Discovery of the Year (2017–2018), the People's Choice prize at Cabaret festif (2018) and a Lucien Award from GAMIQ (2020).

She has practised Québécois step dancing for more than a decade and is artistic director of the Tissés Serrés danse trad school. She travels throughout Quebec and abroad to teach at traditional music camps and workshops.
