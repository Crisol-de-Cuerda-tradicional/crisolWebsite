Laura Wilkie is a fiddle player from Tain in the Scottish Highlands. Her playing is grounded in Highland fiddle traditions but open to a wide range of genres; she is known for an improvisational, exploratory approach and for co-founding **Fat-Suit** (a progressive jazz band). She has toured internationally with **Kinnaris Quintet** and with artists including **Ian Carr**, **Ross Ainslie**, **Niteworks**, **Claire Hastings**, **Beth Malcolm**, **Siobhan Miller**, **Shooglenifty**, **Man of the Minch**, **Rachel Sermanni**, **Elephant Sessions** and **The GRIT Orchestra**, among others.

She is a programmer for Edinburgh Tradfest and takes a particular interest in safeguarding in creative settings and in professional and educational practice.

With **Kinnaris Quintet** she released _Free One_ and _This Too_ (2019, 2022). Her first solo album, _VENT_ (2024), centres on waulking songs—women's work songs from the Highlands and Islands—and reimagines their melodies on the fiddle, drawing on research in Scottish archives and on work with tradition bearer **Rona Lightfoot**. The record explores how those songs have carried a sense of safety, shared knowledge, power, love, humour and freedom to rural working women's communities, and how they resonate for Wilkie as a non-Gaelic speaker engaging with the material.
