Thierry Clouette is a guitarist and multi-instrumentalist from Quebec. He holds a bachelor’s degree in musicology from the Université de Montréal and a diploma in Irish bouzouki from the traditional music program at Cégep de Joliette. In **É.T.É** he performs on guitar and bouzouki alongside fiddler **Élisabeth Moquin** and cellist **Élisabeth Giroux**, shaping the trio’s contemporary take on Québécois and Acadian repertoire with jazz, progressive rock and classical colours. With **É.T.É** he has released *Le boire des minuits* (2017), *Les quatre roses* (2019) and *Sur ces eaux* (2022); the group has toured across North America, Europe and Australia and received honours including a Création Aldor award, an OPUS for Discovery of the Year (2017–2018), the People's Choice prize at Cabaret festif (2018), a Linda Lemay award at the Festival international de la chanson de Granby (2019) and a Lucien Award from GAMIQ (2020).

He has worked as musical director for folk-dance ensembles at international events, and as a record producer and sound engineer for traditional music groups from Quebec. He also leads the progressive folk-trad project **Saligaude**.
